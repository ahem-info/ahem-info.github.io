One-on-One-Support

AHEM Contacts provide information and support and are happy to help new and active homeschoolers with general homeschooling questions, as well as field questions about the Charles guidelines and act as sounding boards for concerns in dealing with school officials. AHEM's resources allow Contacts to have a broad overview and solid understanding of Massachusetts homeschooling guidelines in theory and practice, as well as an awareness of hot spots and trends. AHEM Contacts are experienced homeschooling parent volunteers, not lawyers, and do not offer legal advice.

IMPORTANT: Due to the COVID-19 pandemic we are experiencing a marked increase in the number of people inquiring about homeschooling.

In order to help the greatest number of people possible, before calling an AHEM contact listed below, please check if your question is answered in the FAQ.

The first step to start officially homeschooling in Massachusetts is to submit an education plan to your local school superintendent or school committee. Before picking up the phone to call AHEM, please review the following pages:

  • Homeschool Guidelines at a Glance
  • Tips for Writing Your Education Plan
  • Sample Education Plan
  • Methods of Evaluation
