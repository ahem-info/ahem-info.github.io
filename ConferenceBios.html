Panelists' Bios

Whole Education Without Schooling Conference 2013

Panel: Patchwork of Days
Moderator
  

Milva McDonald has been a homeschooling mom for 22 years. Her children are 28, 26, 16, and 15. During that time, she's been active in her local support group, led book groups, and facilitated writing groups for area homeschoolers. She is also a founding member of AHEM. Milva enjoys reading, writing, singing, socializing, and the Red Sox.

Roberta Altamari has been homeschooling her daughters (ages 11 and 16) their whole lives with a blend of unschooling and eclectic styles. She also works full-time as the director of religious education and youth ministry for First Parish of Sudbury, a Unitarian Universalist congregation. Her homeschooling success is possible because of a respectful co-parenting arrangement with her ex-husband Michael. They both appreciate and value the homeschooling life their daughters are experiencing.

Morgan Kennedy Henderson and her husband Jim have always homeschooled their kids. Jacob is now in his first year at Macalester College in St. Paul, MN; Rhia continues to homeschool into her high school years. They consider themselves to be eclectic in terms of homeschooling philosophy, with experiences ranging from unschooling to being part of a coop to using local community college classes. In addition to enjoying the company of her extended family, Morgan knits, gardens and works part-time at the Mothers' Milk Bank Northeast.

Will Matica is 18 years old. He is a lifelong homeschooler, and has also spent most of his teenage years taking college courses part time. Currently, he is a business major at Greenfield Community College, as well as a working musician. Business and his band are his two passions, and through homeschooling, he has had the opportunity to develop his skills and the dedication to find personal success in the fields that really matter to him.

Originally a native of Florida, Cade Murray graduated from MIT in the mid-90's and then moved to the San Francisco Bay area to work in the semiconductor industry. After being laid-off from a start-up within days of his first daughter being born, he decided to run with it and became a stay-at-home-dad while doing some consulting on the side. When it was time to enter kindergarten, the family decided to give homeschooling a shot, since both Cade and his wife (a physics PhD from Berkeley) felt that their own elementary-school years were largely a waste of time. Everyone loved the way it worked out, and the family has never looked back, using the freedom granted to let his two daughters pursue their own interests extensively and supplementing their education with extended travel across the country and around the globe. After moving back to the Boston area in 2010, Cade and his daughters (now 8 and 10) have gradually become integrated into the vibrant and diverse local homeschool community.

Nadia Sladkey homeschooled her entire life until college. She is now a senior getting her Bachelors in the Science of Nursing at Simmons College in Boston, MA. She is also working as a nurse's assistant at Massachusetts General Hospital. Nadia enjoys cooking for her friends and family, patting every dog she sees on the street, and learning random trivia.

Milva McDonald has been a homeschooling mom for 22 years. Her children are 28, 26, 16, and 15. During that time, she's been active in her local support group, led book groups, and facilitated writing groups for area homeschoolers. She is also a founding member of AHEM. Milva enjoys reading, writing, singing, socializing, and the Red Sox.

Sophia Sayigh lives in Arlington with her husband Rick. Their two children, now 24 and 21, didn't attend school until they went to college. Sophia was active in La Leche League and her local homeschool support group. She is a co-founder of Advocates for Home Education in Massachusetts. An empty nest finds her at work at the public library as a Children's Librarian. Free time is spent cuddling with her pug, spending time with family and friends, reading, cooking and eating, watching movies, walking and otherwise being outdoors. Another of her home-based interests is the home funeral/green burial movement.

Cynthia (Cindy) Turner is originally from the Philly area and has lived in MA for the last 20 years. Her daughters are aged 13 and 9 and her family is in their eighth year without compulsory schooling and will stay that way. She is an advocate at heart and, in addition to working with vulnerable populations, for the past seven years she has made it a point to reach out to others looking at home education as well as to strongly encourage people in general to question the status quo. She has been working with AHEM for almost four years and has been on the Board for two. Cindy enjoys meeting people, singing, walking in the woods, writing, hanging with family and friends, playing games, reading with her girls, road trips, and exploring the new and enriching things that life has to offer.
