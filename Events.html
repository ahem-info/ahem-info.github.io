AHEM’s Assabet River Coop

Welcoming new members for Fall 2018!

If you are:

  • a homeschooling parent 18 years old or older,
  • a Friend of AHEM,
  • homeschooling at least one child born in 2012 or earlier,
  • and live in one of the following towns:
    • Acton
    • Bedford
    • Burlington
    • Carlisle
    • Concord
    • Lexington
    • Lincoln
    • Maynard
    • Stow
    • Sudbury

you are invited to join an established coop designed to help independent homeschoolers get to know each other, share interests and skills, and plan social events, group activities, and field trips together!

The fall session will run from September 13 – November 15, 2018.

The cost is $50/family for the 10 week session.

To join:

  • you must agree to the Coop guidelines, which will be sent to you after you indicate interest and
  • you must commit to attending weekly park days, and to organizing an event or activity for the group, and commit to attending at least one such event.

Communication is via a program written for the purpose of facilitating this network. Members have profiles where they can list interests and skills they are willing to share with others in the group. Members post events that they plan to the group calendar.

If you would like to receive the Handbook and info on how to register, email info@ahem.info. Include your full street address.

Space in the coop is limited. No refunds.

Nuts and Bolts of Homeschooling in Massachusetts - Full

Thursday, July 26, 2018 7:00 - 9:00 pm

Whether you are actively homeschooling or are just curious about homeschool regulations, policies, and procedures, you are invited to attend Nuts and Bolts of Homeschooling in Massachusetts. The free program will be held in Worcester, MA on Thursday, July 26, 2018, 7:00 - 9:00 PM. Precise location will be sent when you register.

This workshop will cover Massachusetts homeschool guidelines; the history of homeschooling in MA; writing your education plan; choosing a form of evaluation; and local homeschool policies. Informational handouts will be available and there will be plenty of time for questions and discussion.

The program will be presented by Amy Faeskorn. Amy is on the board of Advocates for Home Education in Massachusetts, Inc. (AHEM), a non-profit 501(c)(3) charitable educational organization that gathers and disseminates information about homeschooling in Massachusetts through education, advocacy, and events. She is also one of the voices you hear when you call AHEM for help.

Cost is $15/person to reserve your spot; the full price will be refunded to you after the meeting, making your participation FREE. If you don't show up, your money will be donated to support the non-profit mission of AHEM. No refunds except those made to attendees after the workshop on July 26th. 

To preregister, visit https://ahemnutsandbolts.eventbrite.com.

Space is limited and spots are allotted on a first come, first served basis.

Workshop participants will be added to the Friends of AHEM list unless they specifically opt out. Friends of AHEM keep abreast of legislative and other issues that affect Massachusetts homeschoolers via a low volume announcement-only email list. Friends of AHEM get special notice of AHEM events. This is a free service. AHEM respects your privacy and will not share your name or contact information with any other organization.

Please respect AHEM’s mission to empower individuals through education by not soliciting for your business at this event.
