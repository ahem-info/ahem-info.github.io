Getting Started Homeschooling - sterling, MA

Thursday, May 4, 2017, 6:30 - 8:00 pm
Whether you are actively considering home education or are just curious about how it is done, you are invited to attend Getting Started Homeschooling. The free program will be held at the Conant Public Library, 4 Meetinghouse Hill Road, Sterling, MA on Thursday, May 4, 2017, 6:30 - 8:00 pm.

Topics to be covered include an overview of homeschooling, getting started, methods, resources, and homeschooling support. Informational handouts will be available and an opportunity for questions will follow the presentation.

The program will be presented by Milva McDonald and Tracy Ventola. Milva is a founder of Advocates for Home Education in Massachusetts, Inc., a non-profit 501(c)(3) charitable educational organization that gathers and disseminates information about homeschooling in Massachusetts through education, advocacy, and events. Tracy is an AHEM volunteer and a former elementary school teacher now homeschooling her two children.

Admission is free, but preregistration is required. To preregister, call the library at (978) 422-6409 or register at the library.

Space is limited and spots are allotted on a first come, first served basis.

Please respect AHEM’s mission to empower individuals through education by not soliciting for your business at this event.

AHEM's Home Learning Book Club is Back!

Thursday, May 18, 7:30 - 9:00 pm in Chestnut Hill
 Come to a lively discussion of a brand new, debut novel depicting the everyday lives of families exploring the world of unschooling on Thursday, May 18th from 7:30 to 9:00 pm in the café of the Chestnut Hill Wegmans. We are pleased to be joined by Unschoolers co-author and AHEM co-founder Milva McDonald.

Set in the Boston area,Unschoolers offers a glimpse into the rhythms, relationships, challenges, and triumphs of members of Home Learning Together, a support group for new and veteran homeschoolers. A series of connected stories told with humor, honesty, and charm, this book offers a unique perspective on homeschooling not necessarily found in existing literature.

Unschoolers is available in the Minuteman Library Network for loan and can be purchased on Amazon in Kindle and paperback editions.

The Home Learning Book Club, led by AHEM volunteer Amy Faeskorn, (find her at Wegman's in her hot pink AHEM tee shirt) provides homeschooling families with a regular opportunity to read and discuss titles related to learning at home on a wide range of topics. Group members choose books based on the interests of those present at each meeting, and discussions are extremely open and relaxed. Pre-registration is not required.  Anyone is welcome to attend, including those who have not read or finished the chosen book.

“…a dynamic, upbeat novel…vivid characters…In addition to being a fun read, it is also a wonderful portrait of how a community can self-organize and grow through mutual and individual efforts.”
~ Pat Farenga, writer and education activist
