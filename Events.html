Presentation on Dual Enrollment at Middlesex Community College

Monday, June 15th 2:00 pm
Voyagers Homeschool Coop and Resource Center, 530 Main Street, Acton

This presentation is appropriate for families who are ready to take advantage of MCC's program and also for families who just want to learn more, for future reference. Jason Reynolds, Assistant Director of Admissions at Middlesex Community College, will be giving a small presentation explaining the high school Dual Enrollment program, the costs, the opportunities, and the application process. Voyagers members who have attended community college through the dual enrollment program and/or their parents may also be available to answer questions.This presentation is offered free of charge and will be held at Voyagers. Teens who are considering taking classes this fall should definitely attend as well as their parents. RSVPs not required, but appreciated. Home Schooling Community Conference

WHEN: 10 am - 4 pm, Wednesday, June 17

WHO:  Home Schoolers, Anyone Interested in Home Schooling & Anyone Working with the Home Schooling Community

WHAT:  HOW CAN WE HELP EACH OTHER: An Opportunity to Explore Ideas that Require a Community to Make Happen

WHERE:  First Parish Church, 10 Parish Street, Dorchester, MA  02122

We intend this to be the first of regular seasonal gatherings allowing us to meet, share ideas and have time to plan, take stock and work together.

WHY:  Home Schooling takes on many forms, each family defining what it looks like for them. There are aspects of home schooling that we cannot provide on our own: social cohorts, greater purchasing power, organized group activities, long term relationships with other home schoolers, a shared sense of identity - to name a few. If you find yourself looking for these or anything else within your home schooling experience, this gathering creates the space to explore shared interests in order that they may lead to concrete outcomes.

HOW:  OPEN SPACE is a "self organizing" meeting model used throughout the world in business and community settings. It draws directly on the people present to set the agenda and determine outcomes and next steps. The critical elements are: who shows up, what ideas and passions they bring and what they wish to achieve. The OPEN SPACE model allows participants to express their interests and concerns, sort through these and determine concrete next steps to make something happen.

For more details about what the day would look like, please go to: http://www.homeschoolopenspace.org

MORE:  We plan to repeat a more permanent meeting schedule in May, August, November, February to continue this seasonal gathering and planning cycle.

FURTHER DETAILS:

To sign-up, please go to: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/greater-boston-home-schooling-education-open-space-gathering-tickets-16931054239

Registration price includes meeting space and food. Refreshments (coffee/tea & baked goods from Dot2Dot Cafe), lunch (vegetarian rice, beans & salad) and snacks will be served.

A "Family" ticket covers one - four people. For families larger than four, please select "Plus One" per person in addition to "Family" ticket.

Children are welcome. Activities for children will be provided.  Childcare for children 5 and under available upon request for shared cost of the sitter. (Please select "Childcare" from ticket options.)

Ample parking is available.  10-15 minute from Fields Corner T on the Red Line.

If cost is an issue, please contact us.  We do not wish for someone not to attend owing to cost.

Please reach out with any questions to Lucia, luciaopenspace at gmail dot com.

Home Learning Book Club - Burlington

Tuesday, June 23, 7:30 pm

Our next book is Free to Learn: Five Ideas for a Joyful Unschooling Life by Pam Laricchia. We will meet on Tuesday, June 23 in the café of the Burlington Wegmans at 7:30 p.m.

The Home Learning Book Club, led by AHEM volunteer Amy Faeskorn, provides homeschooling families with a regular opportunity to read and discuss titles related to learning at home on a wide range of topics. Group members choose books based on the interests of those present at each meeting, and discussions are extremely open and relaxed. Pre-registration is not required. Anyone is welcome to attend, including those who have not read or finished the chosen book.

After this meeting, Book Club will break for the summer and reconvene in the fall. Hope you can join us on June 23rd!
